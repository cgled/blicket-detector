Shape Perception

When faced with a decision-making task, an individual's attention may be anchored to certain salient pieces of information. In order to design decision-making tasks, researchers have to account for certain personal variables that may be related to the information one uses in the decision process. As such, we are interested in learning more about certain factors that may relate to how you, as a decision-maker, process complex information. Specifically, this question was designed to examine whether participants take the time to read the instructions presented to them.
